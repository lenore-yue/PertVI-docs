PertVI: Disentangled Perturbation Variational Inference

1. Introduction

Understanding how pharmacological perturbations drive cellular state changes remains a core challenge in single-cell transcriptomics. Standard deep generative models typically compress all transcriptomic variance into a single monolithic latent space. This approach suffers from two critical flaws. First, it allows highly covariant biological processes, such as the cell cycle, to overwhelm and dominate the latent embedding. Second, it treats single-cell perturbations as isolated events, mathematically conflating the cell's underlying baseline biology with the specific mechanism of action of the drug.

To address this, we developed Disentangled Perturbation Variational Inference (PertVI), a structural causal architecture designed to explicitly partition these entangled processes through multi-encoder isolation and transcriptomic momentum modeling. By learning the natural baseline and limiting the drug to an affine shift along that baseline, PertVI allows us to observe how a perturbation actively pushes a cell along permitted biological trajectories, while reserving a dedicated out-of-distribution space strictly for off-manifold stress or toxicity.

Availability Note: The core architecture of PertVI is implemented as a native PyTorch framework within the scvi-tools ecosystem[1]. Full open-source access will be released concurrently with the foundational manuscript on bioRxiv.

2. Architecture

PertVI replaces standard VAE compression with a semantic filtration system. The architecture routes data through three distinct stages to enforce causal disentanglement.

The Encoder Stage. The model receives the raw transcriptomic matrix () and routes it through three parallel encoders. To function as unbiased feature extractors, these encoders are strictly blind to the perturbation labels.

  • Topological Anchor (): A low-dimensional space designed to map the continuous, periodic trajectory of the cell cycle.
  • Macroscopic Anchor (): A medium-dimensional space representing the universal biological manifold (the stable differentiation or metabolic backbone).
  • Anomaly Escape Hatch (): A restrictive bottleneck reserved exclusively for capturing residual, off-manifold perturbation effects that the natural anchors cannot explain.

The Transition Stage. To calculate transcriptomic momentum, the perturbation identifier is embedded and applied exclusively to the on-manifold anchors using Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM)[2], leading to the construction of spaces.

The Decoder Stage. To ensure the latent manifolds remain perfectly batch-invariant while isolating the biological pathways, the final pre-activation logits are calculated via a unified summation:

  • Linear Cycle Head (): Decodes strictly from . It is governed by a Hard Mask, restricting its weight matrix to only map to pre-specified core cell cycle genes.
  • Dense Main Head (): An MLP decoding from the concatenated state and OOD vectors ().
  • Batch Bypass (): A learned bias vector correcting for technical covariates (e.g., sequencing depth), applied symmetrically to all genes.

3. Mathematics

The structural boundaries of PertVI are enforced by targeted penalties within the modified Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) objective.

Information Bottlenecks (KL Priors). We utilize information bottlenecks to guide and constrain the behavior of encoders to learn the universal, perturbation-agnostic biogical manifolds.

  • Topological Relaxation. Specifically, we apply a fractional weight () to the cell cycle KL term. This gives the model the mathematical slack necessary to map the continuous periodic topology of cell division.
  • Asymmetric OOD KL. To capture the perturbation-induced toxicity/stress response, we utilize asymmetric KL priors for OOD encoder. Specifically, for control cells, we anchor the mean to zero: . We then let the treated cells face the standard prior .

Semantic Isolation Penalties. To prevent variance leakage across the independent pathways, we utilize three distinct penalty system.

  • Conditional OOD Crush. To mathematically define the origin as the unperturbed baseline, we apply an penalty exclusively to the predicted mean of the control cells.

  • Soft L1 Decoder Mask. To prevent the Dense Main Head () from smuggling cell cycle variance to lower its reconstruction loss, we penalize the specific subset of its input weights () that connect to the core cell cycle genes.

  • Cross-Covariance Repulsion. We penalize the squared Frobenius norm of the cross-covariance matrices to enforce strict orthogonality between distinct biological programs.

4. API Reference & Usage

PertVI is fully integrated into the scvi-tools ecosystem[1-1] with user-friendly interface for data registration, model training, and latent extraction.

Model Setup and Training Example. The workflow mimics standard single-cell generative modeling pipelines. Crucially, the data registration step requires explicit annotation of the perturbation label (sample_key), the control/treatment mask (treatment_mask_key to govern the origin), and the cell cycle gene mask (cycle_gene_mask_key).

import scanpy as sc
from pertvi import PertVI

# 1. Load annotated dataset
adata = sc.read_h5ad("tcell_perturbation_screen.h5ad")

# 2. Register AnnData with PertVI specific fields
PertVI.setup_anndata(
    adata,
    layer="counts",
    batch_key="plate_id",                  # Technical covariates
    sample_key="compound_id",              # Perturbation labels (used for FiLM conditioning)
    treatment_mask_key="is_treated",       # Binary mask (0 for DMSO, 1 for perturbed)
)

# 3. Initialize the model with defined structural bottlenecks
model = PertVI(
    adata,
    n_latent_cycle=3,   # Topological anchor for periodic cell cycle
    n_latent_state=20,  # Macroscopic anchor for intrinsic differentiation
    n_latent_ood=10,     # Restrictive escape hatch for off-manifold novelty
    cycle_gene_mask_key="is_cycle_gene"    # Boolean mask for the core cycle genes
)

# 4. Execute training with dynamic penalty scheduling
model.train(
    max_epochs=400,
    warmup_epochs=50,
    max_lambda_adv=10.0, # Cross-covariance repulsion weight
    max_lambda_ood=50.0  # L1 penalty weight for the OOD space
)

# 5. Extract the decoupled semantic spaces for downstream analysis
latent_dict = model.get_latent_representation()
adata.obsm["X_u_cycle"] = latent_dict["u_cycle"]     # Perturbation-agnostic cell cycle baseline manifold
adata.obsm["X_u_state"] = latent_dict["u_state"]     # Perturbation-agnostic state baseline manifold
adata.obsm["X_z_state"] = latent_dict["z_shift"]     # Transcriptomic momentum on state manifold
adata.obsm["X_z_shift"] = latent_dict["z_shift"]     # Merged transcriptomic momentum
adata.obsm["X_v_ood"] = latent_dict["v_ood"]         # Off-manifold toxicity/stress

Downstream Analytical Modules. PertVI explicitly models a structural causal graph. To analyze the outputs, we designed targeted analytical modules to interrogate the distinct mathematical phenomena captured within each latent space. The package includes a dedicated downstream analysis API (pertvi.tl).

5. Benchmarking

To empirically validate the causal disentanglement capabilities of PertVI, we benchmarked the architecture against standard methods , conventional data integration tools (e.g., COMBAT[3]), and state-of-the-art generative models (e.g., CPA[4], MrVI[5]).

The evaluation was performed on a high-throughput in vitro pharmacological screen of human primary CD8+ T cells[6], containing highly entangled variances from technical batches, natural differentiation trajectories, cell cycle progression, and over 60 distinct pharmacological perturbations.

Benchmark 1: Batch Handling

Like conventional state-of-the-art models (e.g., CPA and MrVI), PertVI integrates technical covariate correction natively into its causal framework. Crucially, unlike standard statistical approaches such as COMBAT, PertVI prevents the destruction of underlying biological manifold caused by overcorrection during the batch correction. This is particularly critical when handling single-cell perturbation data, where technical batch effects are often highly entangled with perturbation effects.

Since the PertVI encoders explicitly strip technical artifacts during the initial compression phase, both the perturbation-agnostic anchors (, ) and the perturbation-aware spaces (, , , ) remain completely free of batch effects, reflecting only genuine phenotypic and pharmacological variance. For expression-based downstream analysis, users can use PertVI.get_normalized_expression(transform_batch="...") to obtain the harmonized transcriptomic profiles.

Benchmark 2: Trajecory Disentanglement

B2.1 Cell cycle

A fundamental challenge in modeling single-cell perturbation data is the tendency for highly covariant signals, such as cell cycle progression, to entangle with and overwhelm intrinsic biological processes. Here, PertVI utilizes a dedicated, specialized encoder to explicitly capture cell cycle dynamics. The resulting perturbation-agnostic space successfully reconstructs the continuous, periodic topology of the cell cycle. This isolated space will serve as a natural biological anchor, allowing us to independently evaluate how specific perturbations arrest or accelerate cell division.

Crucially, by physically quarantining this variance, the macroscopic state encoder is now freed to accurately reconstruct the pristine cell differentiation trajectory (B2.2). In contrast, the latent embeddings generated by conventional deep generative models, such as CPA (even with cell cycle phase trained as covariates) and MrVI, remain heavily dominated by cell cycle effects, which can obscure the true underlying developmental state of the cell.

Interestingly, when we visualize PertVI's final concatenated representation (), the resulting embedding exhibits similar entangled topology seen in CPA and MrVI. Given that merges the cell cycle and cell differentiation variances back together prior to decoding, this observation empirically confirms that these distinct biological programs naturally entangle when forced into a single unified latent space, proving that upstream structural disentanglement is necessary to extract biologically meaningful manifolds.

B2.2 Cell state

With cell cycle effects being isolated, the macroscopic state encoder in PertVI provides an unpolluted foundation for mapping intrinsic cellular differentiation. Specifically, PertVI's perturbation-agnostic manifold enables a distinctly clearer separation and robust annotation of T cell subsets across the effector differentiation continuum, seamlessly resolving the naive, proliferating, memory-like, effector, and terminal effector populations.

In constrast,the natural developmental transitions between T cell states are obscured in CPA and MrVI due to the dominance of cell cycle effect in the latent spaces. Furthermore, standard integration methods like COMBAT fail entirely in this scenario; their aggressive covariate regression leads to severe overcorrection, which shatters the sequential, evolutionary relationships between T cell populations.

Benchmark 3: Isolation of Perturbation Effect

B3.1 All perturbations

A defining feature of PertVI is its mathematical representation of a perturbation as an affine shift (, ) acting strictly upon the unperturbed biological baseline (, ). Standard integration methods like LDA and COMBAT can detect broad perturbation signals, but they irretrievably entangle these effects with intrinsic biology, which ultimately destroys the underlying developmental topology in latent embeddings. Conversely, SOTA generative model MrVI, while being designed to construct multi-resolution representations that separate sample-unaware and sample-aware variances, fails to systematically isolate perturbation signals when compounds are processed as sample covariates. This failure is likely due to the fact that a single dominant perturbagen in a high-throughput screen can monopolize the variance of the sample-aware latent space, effectively masking the nuanced, biologically significant signals of other compounds.

PertVI, in constrast, overcomes this bottleneck through its perturbation-agnostic spaces and the FiLM transition module. This architecture provides a highly sensitive, isolated recapitulation of individual perturbation effects, preventing dominant signals from overshadowing the rest of the screen. Crucially, the bifurcated FiLM mechanism enables PertVI to calculate transcriptomic momentum independently, explicitly separating a drug's shifting effect on the periodic cell cycle manifold from its shifting effect on the continuous cell differentiation manifold .

B3.2 Representative cases

Here are some examples to show how different perturbation effects are captured by PertVI versus other approaches. DMSO, the control. Decitabine, a known DNA methyltransferase inhibitor that can promote memory-like effector phenotype and improve T cell persistency in CAR-T therapy[7]. Thapsigargin, a known calcium channel pump inhibitor that can promote T cell activation and effector differentiation[8]. Ruxolitinib, a known JAK1/2 inhibitor utilized in T cell lymphoma treatment that suppresses T cell activation, migration, and the secretion of pro-inflammatory cytokines[9].

B3.3 On-manifold and off-manifold effects

Importantly, PertVI leverages its perturbation-agnostic anchors (, ) and the anomaly escape hatch () to systematically map on-manifold developmental shifts versus off-manifold toxicity. By performing differential density enrichment analysis against the control baseline (e.g., DMSO, non-targeting gRNAs), we can precisely dissect complex mechanistic phenotypes.

For instance, followed by B3.2, although both Ruxolitinib and Thapsigargin induce profound G1 cell cycle arrest, mapping their treated cells onto the intrinsic manifold reveals starkly divergent differentiation trajectories: Ruxolitinib forcefully retains cells in a naive state, whereas Thapsigargin largely enriches the terminal effector populations. Simultaneously, their distinct topological footprints in the space mathematically confirm that these compounds trigger entirely independent off-target stress responses.

In sharp contrast, cells treated with Decitabine exhibit targeted enrichment toward an IL7R+ memory-like phenotype on the biological manifold, but remain close to the center where DMSO controls located in the space. This distinct lack of off-manifold deviation, combined with a highly conserved transcriptomic momentum profile shown in in B3.2, suggests that Decitabine does not induce too much unnatural cellular stress; rather, it acts as a transcriptomic catalyst to naturally accelerate a permitted developmental pathway.

Together, by structurally bifurcating permitted biological momentum from aberrant toxicity, PertVI provides a unique level of mechanistic granularity that complements the response prediction and data integration capabilities of conventional single-cell approaches.
